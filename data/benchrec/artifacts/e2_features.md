## Why stratification matters (fix 7)
The **same** `qgram3_jaccard` feature over **all cardinalities** (1:1 + N:M mixed) scores dev 98.75% (LB 98.55%) / val 99.29% (LB 99.02%) - far higher, because N:M diagonal blocks are textually easy. An unstratified ablation would have reported that inflated number and credited text with a strength it does not have on the 1:1 job.

## E2 conclusion
- On genuinely ambiguous **1:1** blocks, the best feature (**qgram3_jaccard**, selected on dev) disambiguates at only **64.47% (LB 55.09%)** on val - a **modest** lever, barely above the `desc_sim` baseline (val 59.21% (LB 49.78%)), on a small population (val n=76).
- Character features do beat semantic-embedding intuition and edge out the baseline, but the honest takeaway is that **text is a minor contributor** to the 1:1 path; the major levers remain the reciprocal-unique rule (E1) and correct N:M handling (E4).
- The high all-cardinality score suggests text may be more useful for **N:M candidate selection** - to be measured in E4, not assumed here.
- ~10% of ambiguous 1:1 B's have empty attributes -> no text signal -> review.
